## 🔺 The CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is the **foundation of every cybersecurity decision**. It defines three core security goals that every system, organization, and individual must protect.

```
          🔒 CONFIDENTIALITY
                  /\
                 /  \
                /    \
               /      \
              /________\
   🛡️ INTEGRITY    📡 AVAILABILITY
```

---

### 🔒 1. Confidentiality
> *Only authorized people can access sensitive information.*

**What it means:** Data must be protected from unauthorized access, disclosure, or exposure.

**Real-life example from my life:**
- My **banking app login credentials** and UPI PIN must remain private. If exposed, an attacker could drain my account without my knowledge.

**Controls that protect it:**
- Strong passwords + Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- End-to-end encryption (WhatsApp, Signal)
- Locking devices with biometric/PIN

---

### 🛡️ 2. Integrity
> *Data must remain accurate, consistent, and untampered.*

**What it means:** Information must not be modified by unauthorized parties, either in storage or in transit.

**Real-life example from my life:**
- My **academic records and project grades** at BCU must stay accurate. A malicious actor tampering with my marks database would destroy trust in the system.

**Controls that protect it:**
- Hashing (SHA-256) to detect tampering
- Digital signatures on important documents
- Role-based access control (RBAC) for edit permissions

---

### 📡 3. Availability
> *Systems and data must be accessible when needed.*

**What it means:** Authorized users must be able to access information and services reliably and on time.

**Real-life example from my life:**
- **BCU exam portal and college LMS** must be available during exam season. A DDoS attack or server crash during result time causes real harm to students.

**Controls that protect it:**
- Regular backups (cloud + local)
- Redundant systems and load balancing
- DDoS protection and uptime monitoring

## ⚠️ 3 Threats to My Personal Data

### 🎣 Threat 1 — Phishing Attacks
| | Details |
|--|--|
| **What it is** | Fake emails, SMS, or websites impersonating trusted services |
| **How it targets me** | A fake Google login page or fake bank alert could steal my credentials |
| **Impact** | Account takeover, financial loss, identity theft |
| **My defense** | Verify sender, never click unknown links, enable MFA on all accounts |

---

### 📱 Threat 2 — Device Loss or Theft
| | Details |
|--|--|
| **What it is** | Physical theft of phone or laptop without strong security |
| **How it targets me** | If stolen without encryption/PIN, all saved passwords, photos, and files are exposed |
| **Impact** | Data breach, unauthorized account access, privacy violation |
| **My defense** | Screen lock + biometrics, device encryption, remote wipe via Google Find My Device |

---

### 🔑 Threat 3 — Weak Password Hygiene
| | Details |
|--|--|
| **What it is** | Reusing the same weak password across multiple accounts |
| **How it targets me** | If one account is leaked (e.g., data breach), all linked accounts are compromised |
| **Impact** | Multiple account takeovers through credential stuffing |
| **My defense** | Use a password manager, unique passwords per site, check breaches via HaveIBeenPwned |

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- The **CIA Triad** is not just theory — it applies to everyday digital life.
- Every threat can be mapped back to breaking **Confidentiality**, **Integrity**, or **Availability**.
- A strong **security mindset** is the first and most important control.
- Even without advanced tools, **awareness + habits** can prevent 90% of attacks.
